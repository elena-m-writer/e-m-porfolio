Wildlife

I've grown up in a family that has always paid attention to wildlife and horticulture. If you can't identify common garden birds and plants when you meet us, you will once you've known us a few weeks! There's nothing I like more than looking out at our own garden seeing what's flitting in and out, you never quite know who's going to pay you a visit.
This year will be my first year growing vegetables from seed, and come the end of May everything I have should be in a pot ready for the makeshift cold-frame. If the pandemic has taught me anything it's that we need nature now more than ever, so it's time to get growing, get birdwatching, and generally just get a bit more wild!

Yoga

Since trying to get fit after I left school in 2014 I've been dipping in and out of yoga - and no, it really isn't 'just stretching'. Yoga is great for feeling and understanding what your body can do, and it can relax your mind by forcing you to focus specifically on what your body is doing in the here and now!
